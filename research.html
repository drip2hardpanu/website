Lubbe Lab '22-'24

Human Genomics Lab at Northwestern Medicine studying genetic causes of motion-debilitating disease. PI: Dr. Steven Lubbe

  • On the Relationship Between Rare Variants and Polygenic Risk Scoring
  • Study focused on finding rare-variant contributors to Parkinsons' Disease as their low frequency makes them prone to marginalization in summary statistics.
    1. Scoring of individuals in dataset using meta-5 summary statistics. Scoring was done via PRSice. IDs of affected individuals with low PRS scores were stored

      2. Quantile plot generated from PRS Scoring

    2. CADD annotations done via annovar. Coding variants with high CADD scores (i.e., > 12.37) were moved into another file, along with variants annotated as nonsense SNPs, splice site errors, and indels.
    3. Quality-control on ampPD genetic data using PLINK. Filtered out variants with MAF > .05. Reintroduced variants from step 2 into dataset
    4. Rare Variant Analysis done via SkatO
  • On Genetic Contributers to Early-Onset Alzheimers Disease
  • Genome-Wide Association aiming to elucidate rare variant contributors to Early-Onset Alzhemiers Disease.
  • On Genetic Causes of Parkinsons'
  • Training study. Genome-wide association study to uncover genetic causes of Parkinsons' disease
